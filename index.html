Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1968

Intel Founded

Intel is founded in Mountain View, California, by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore. The company would go on to become a leader in semiconductor innovation, shaping the future of technology worldwide. Early products included memory chips that set the stage for the microprocessor revolution.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing devices.

1971

First Microprocessor

The Intel 4004 contains 2,300 transistors and performs 60,000 operations per second, revolutionizing the design of computers and electronics. This innovation paved the way for the development of more powerful and compact computing devices.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 microprocessor introduces the x86 instruction set, which becomes the foundation for most personal computers for decades to come. Its architecture is still present in modern CPUs, making it a pivotal moment in computing history.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

1985

386 Processor

The 386 processor enables advanced multitasking and memory management, paving the way for modern operating systems and applications. Its 32-bit design allowed for more powerful and flexible software.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. Over subsequent years, Intel invests heavily in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse this trend.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

In 2006, Intel's operational greenhouse gas emissions peaked. The company responded by investing in advanced chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing, setting a new standard for environmental responsibility in the tech industry.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals, aiming to drive industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

2020

RISE Strategy

The RISE strategy set ambitious 2030 goals for climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction. Intel committed to responsible, inclusive, and sustainable practices, driving industry-wide progress and innovation.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announces its commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040, building on years of environmental initiatives.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040, building on a legacy of environmental initiatives and leadership in sustainability.

2023

Renewable Electricity

The company achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, helping to drastically lower carbon emissions and driving progress toward Intel's long-term sustainability goals.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, drastically lowering carbon emissions and making significant progress toward its long-term sustainability goals. This milestone demonstrates Intel's commitment to environmental responsibility.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

2024

Sustainability Summit

The first Intel Sustainability Summit brings together key stakeholders from across the industry to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing, setting new standards for environmental progress.
